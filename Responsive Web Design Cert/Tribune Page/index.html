Dr. Dijkstra's Life Timeline:

1930: Born on the 11th of May in Rotterdam, Netherlands

1952: Became Netherland's first "programmer"

1956: Formulated and solved the shortest path problem (not published until 1959)

1959: Received his PhD from the University of Amsterdam

1960: Developed the first compiler for the programming language ALGOL 60 with his partner Jaap Zonneveld

1972: Won the Turning Award

1974: Won the Harry H. Goode Memorial Award from the IEEE Computer Society

1976: Received honorary Doctorate of Science from the Queen's University Belfast

1984: Accepted role at The University of Texas

1989: Recieved ACM/SIGCSE Award for Outstanding Contributions to Computer Science Education

1999: Retired

2001: Revieved honorary doctorate from the Athens University of Economics & Business, Greece

2002: Passed away on the 6th of August at the age of 72
